Necessity of Fake News Detection

Fake news refers to false or misleading information, including disinformation, propaganda, and hoaxes, presented as news with the aim of damaging the reputation of a person or entity or making money through advertising revenue.0 It is defined as news stories that are fabricated, with no verifiable facts, sources, or quotes. Sometimes these stories may be propaganda intentionally designed to mislead the reader or may be designed as "clickbait" written for economic incentives. Fake news is a complex and nuanced problem that is far greater than the narrow definition above.

In an era dominated by digital information, the necessity of fake news detection has never been more critical. Our society's well-being depends on accurate information, making it imperative to preserve truth and accuracy. Beyond individual empowerment, fake news detection plays a pivotal role in protecting public discourse, preventing social division, and upholding media credibility. Moreover, in the realm of national security, countering misinformation is essential to safeguarding the interests of nations.
